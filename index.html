  6. Warriors Orochi 3

    7. Combining the worlds of Samurai Warriors in Sengoku Japan, (Characters from Samurai Warriors 3, and the cut characters from Samurai Warriors 1 and 2) and Dynasty Warriors in Three Kingdoms China, (Characters from Dynasty Warriors 7) while mixing in a few bonus characters, like Orochi, Orochi X, Gyuki, Diamondback, Dodomeki, Da Ji, Sun Wukong, Kiyomori Taira, Himiko, Kyubi/Tamamo, Susano'o, Mystics like Kaguya and Taigong Wang, (even though there's many more) Semei Abe, and many other characters in Koei's (the people who made the Warriors Games) other games, too. Not only that, two extra historical characters come into play, as well. Joan of Arc and Achilles are in this game, as well. This game has the best storyline in the Warriors Orochi series, in my opinion. With over 150 characters to play, with extremely fun gameplay, fun storyline, and just a really fun game.

    You can find more info about Warriors Orochi 3 here.

  7. Totally Accurate Battle Simulator

    8. This game is both fun and really stupid. It is a battle simulator, but the "Totally Accurate" part is debatable. You could pit cavemen against Ancient Greece, or Samurai against 5 wooly mammoths. There's multiple factions, a unit creator, faction creator, map creator, and a campaign creator. The gameplay is really ridiculous, but hilarious at the same time. Your units could be randomized in size, looks, (like being a cyclops, having two eyes, or having the same amount of eyes as a spider, having a big or small head, and a whole bunch of other characteristics.) The ridiculous parts is where your units could trip over a blade of grass and fall into a coma, wield their weapons weirdly, and many ridiculous other things. The game's also really cheap, too. It's around like $23, and I would highly recommend that you get it.

    You can find more stuff about it here.

Favourite Movies

  1. Back To The Future 1-3

    2. My favourite movie series of all time. The original released in 1985, and it focuses on two characters: Marty McFly, and Doc Brown. Doc Brown invents a time machine out of a DeLorean car. It requires 1.28 gigawatts to power the flux capacator, which makes time travel possible. Not only that, you also gotta travel at 88 mph in order to travel through time. The plots in all three of those movies are amazing. In the first movie, Marty travels to 1955 to save Doc Brown after he gets killed in 1985, and Marty meets his parents, but younger. In the second movie, Marty and Doc Brown travels to 2015 to make sure Marty's and Jennifer's kids don't get into jail, but then an alternate timeline happens when George McFly's bully, Biff Tannen uses something from the future to get himself rich and marrying Marty's mother. Marty fixes the timeline, but Doc Brown's time machine gets struck by lightning and travels to 1885. Marty wants to go back to 1885 to save Doc Brown, even though the letter sent to Marty written by Doc told him explicitly to go back to 1985, as he's happy with his life in 1885. This movie series has both comedy and really great action sequences, as well.

    You can find more about the franchise here.
